Jimmy Strawn in his Market District apartment, where he had lived since the 1940s. From the NYS Archives.

“….tearing down ‘these 50 and 60-year-old buildings might make for some decent places in which to live.’”

 

Lillian Pierce owned the Pierce Hotel along with several other Rooming House District residential properties. Courtesy of the Times Union.

“How is it going to help downtown? Naturally, we don’t want to move. Even the news may scare my people away. And they’ve been coming here for years.”

Rev. George Gallas was pastor of St. Sophia Greek Orthodox Church, which was in the process of expanding. From the NYS Archives.

“Up until last night, the plans were going through. We had bought up 10 pieces of property. We knocked down buildings. Then today we read the papers. It was a blockbuster.”

Pharmacist Ken Hunter had been in business up the block from the Cathedral since the Great Depression. From the NYS Archives.
Read the Personal Story: Hunter's Pharmacy

“I’ve been here 33 years this November. I’ve established a very good business and I would like to finish my life of work here. It would be too great a hardship to try and start over again. Why don’t they just take the property that had deteriorated and leave the good paying businesses and properties alone?”

Domenico Ingemie owned this house near the Cathedral with his wife Venera. From the Albany Institute.

“We have lived in this house for 35 years. It is all we have. We are not of the age where we can be put out of our home and start all over again. How are we going to get accustomed to or make new friends if we have to move out of an area that all our friends are in.”

Down the block from Alice Stoutenburgh’s Lancaster Street home in the Rooming House District. From the NYS Archives.

“We bought this place for taxes 20 years ago. This was a respectable neighborhood then. Now a woman doesn’t dare go out on the street alone. It’s a disgrace to the capital city…. My house is kept in good repair but I’m fighting a losing battle on this street. Even before this happened, I’d been looking for a two-family home in some other part of the city.”
